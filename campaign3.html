The Problem

Teenagers are struggling with acne, pimples, and oily skin, leading to insecurities and low self-esteem. Mothers are concerned about their daughters feeling unhappy, insecure, and moody because of these skin issues.

The Opportunity

The campaign helps teenage girls feel confident in their skin with Ibomvu red clay while fostering sisterhood. It promotes positivity and empowerment among young women. Linked to Heritage Day, it celebrates its cultural roots and the tradition of African red clay.

Key Insight

Having acne, dark spots, and blemishes makes the youth, especially young girls, feel unhappy about their appearance because they don't have a solution.

Target Audience

The primary target audience is girls aged 12 to 19 with skin issues, who value natural skincare and empowerment. The secondary audience is their mothers, who will purchase the products and offer support. Both groups prioritize self-care and sustainability.
